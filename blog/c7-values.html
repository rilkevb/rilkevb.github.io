DBC: Reflecting on Values

11/23/14

DBC asked us to think about and reflect on a few questions about our personal values. My answers are the following:

1.) When I reflect back to times in my life when I have been the happiest and the most satisfied many of these instances embodied a few of the same values. In no particular order, these values are awareness, integrity, curiosity, understanding, play, love, kindness, and calm.

2.) Although, it is not explicitly stated on the list of values, I think awareness is one of the most important values for me. Awareness is being aware of who I am. This includes my thoughts, my emotions, my belief framework, and how these are interacting. It also includes being aware of where I am. This includes how the environment and the people I am spending my time with are affecting and being affected by me. I see awareness as being awake to life and truth. Without it, it is very easy to descend into self-delusion and distraction which will waste the precious years I have left. I know I will never be fully aware (what would this even mean?) but I see it as something I should aspire towards.

3.) I try to live up to these values as much as I can but there is always room for improvement.

4.) The last topic someone asked me for advice on was how to be more effective when studying Ruby and Javascript.

5.) When I think about my values it serves to clarify what is important to me but also show how much further I need to go. Also, when confronting challenging situations I know that reflecting on my values helps me focus on what I want and power through the difficult times.
