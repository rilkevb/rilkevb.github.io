Thinking About Learning at DBC

10/25/14

DBC requires new students to take a quiz identifying their "Gregorc Thinking Style". (1) This 'model', using two bits of information (2), divides all learners into 4 different thinking categories. After taking the quiz I was basically tied between 3 different categories: 'Concrete Sequential', 'Concrete Random', and 'Abstract Sequential'. The supplied interpretation guide tells you what these different categories mean. Among many other things, I discovered that I like:

  • "applying logic in solving or finding solutions to problems"
  • "experimenting to find answers"
  • "analyzing situations before making a decision or acting".
  • "using my intuition"(3)

Pairing these insights with my own observations about how I learn has helped me optimize my learning in the first 3 weeks of Phase 0.

Of course, there is still a great deal of room for improvement. Effectively managing my time and organizing what I'm learning are areas where I want to improve. Using a modified 'pomodoro technique' has really helped me structure my time and remember to take regular breaks. I want to continue using it daily. Also, while I inefrequently wrote and almost never reviewed class notes before DBC, I've found that methodically typing out each new HTML/CSS/Ruby element and then reading over these notes at the end of the day has really helped me internalize all this new information.

I want to keep looking for ways to broaden and improve my ability to learn. For this coming week, I want to work on taking a minute or two to think about programming problems in the abstract before immediatly diving into the code. I hope this will encourage me to think about the big picture and arrive at more elegant, interdependent solutions.

1. A 'model' for 'how people learn' presented as empirical fact although, like most 'learning models', there appears to be almost no supporting research/studies to explain the validity of the proposed divisions and it is debatable if a falsifiable hypothesis could even be presented within the framework.

2. I'm not sure if this is enough information to describe the diversity how humans approach the world in any meaningful way.

3. Which requires an incredibly charitable interpretation to not appear as completely conflicting statements.
